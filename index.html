Why Should I Clean My Dryer Vent?

Lint and other debris can build up in your dryer hose and vent duct, reducing air flow, backing up exhaust gases and eventually creating a fire. Annually there are around 18,000 dryer vent fires in the U.S. These hazards can be avoided by thoroughly inspecting and cleaning your dryer vent every year. A blocked vent or vent with restricted airflow shortens the life of your dryer. It can lead to costly repairs if the fan , blower , or heating element burns out. A clogged dryer vent wastes more electric or natural gas to dry your clothes. You should never need to dry your clothes several times to get them dry.
